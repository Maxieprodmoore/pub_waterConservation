How much water do we have?

Ever wondered how much each national tap adds to our water supplies? Click to find out more!

Imported Water from our nearest neighbour alongside rain water make up to 35% of our water sources currently. NEWater make up to 40% of our water sources currently. Desalinated water treated make up to 25% of our water sources currently.

Consequences of No Water

Click on the images in the collage to see the consequences of having no water.

We lose the things that make up our beautiful landscapes.

We lose our sources of hydration and eventually die of thirst.

Plants will wither and die, and the soil gets eroded without the plants roots to anchor them.

Certain animals will lose their food sources.

Creatures of the water will lose their habitats.

Protecting Singapore's Water

Sources of Water in Singapore

Ever wondered what happens to the water before it is pumped into Singapore's water supply?

Click/Hover over the images to find out more!

Water catchments and Drainages

Water is collected from the water catchment areas and drains and sent for treatment via the water pipes.

Water Treatment

At the water treatment plants, water is chemically treated, filtered and disinfected, thereby making it safe for drinking.

Water Tanks

Water is then channeled to the water tanks, ready to be pumped into our reservoirs.

Reservoirs

Water is finally pumped out for our use at home or in our industries.
